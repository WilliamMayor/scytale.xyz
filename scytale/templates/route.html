Route Cipher

Route ciphers are interesting transposition ciphers that involve reading letters out of a grid in any route at all. Unlike other transposition ciphers this cipher is very flexible, any route can be used, as long as you can remember it.

The key for a route cipher is the dimensions of the grid you write letters in to, and the route you use to read them out.

To encrypt a message write it into a grid of your specified dimensions. Then read the letters out as you travel your route through the grid. For example, you could use a 5x5 grid and travel a spiral route from the top left, clockwise towards the centre. The possibilities are nearly endless.

To decrypt a message write it back into a grid of the correct dimensions along the route. Then read out the letters left to right.

Mixed Alphabet Cipher

The Mixed Alphabet Cipher is a very simple substitution cipher that simply replaces letters in the plain text with other letters according to a key. Each plain letter is always replaced by the same cipher letter (e.g. the letter 'a' might always encrypt to the letter 'J'). To decrypt you simply use the key in reverse to undo the first replacement.
